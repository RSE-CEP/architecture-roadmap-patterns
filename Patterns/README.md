# Patterns

This folder contains Research Software Engineering (RSE) patterns for the Humanities, Arts, Social Sciences (HASS) and Indigenous research community.

## Overview

Patterns describe recurring issues in research software engineering and provide proven solutions that balance competing requirements. These patterns help researchers, RSEs, and software engineers make informed technical decisions that respect both technical excellence and research values.

## What is a Pattern?

A **pattern** describes an issue that occurs repeatedly in our environment, and then describes the core of the solution, in such a way that you can use this solution a million times over, without ever doing it the same way twice.

Every pattern consists of three fundamental parts:
1. **Context** - The situation where the issue exists
2. **Issues** - The conflicting requirements that must be balanced
3. **Solution** - A configuration that balances the system issues

## Pattern Categories

Patterns in this collection address:

- Software development practices
- Infrastructure and deployment

### Research Patterns
- Reproducibility and transparency
- Collaboration and sharing
- Documentation and preservation
- Data publication and citation

### Ethical and Cultural Patterns
- Indigenous data sovereignty
- FAIR principles implementation
- CARE principles for Indigenous data
- Privacy and confidentiality
- Responsible AI and LLM use

## Using These Patterns

Patterns are not rigid prescriptions but flexible guides:

- **Adapt** patterns to your specific context
- **Combine** multiple patterns to address complex situations
- **Learn** from the trade-offs discussed in each pattern
- **Contribute** your own variants and experiences

## Pattern Structure

Each pattern follows a consistent structure:
- **Name** - A descriptive title
- **Context** - When and where this pattern applies
- **Issues/Forces** - Competing concerns to balance
- **Solution** - The core approach
- **Consequences** - Trade-offs and implications
- **Examples** - Real-world applications
- **Related Patterns** - Connected patterns

## Special Considerations

### Indigenous Cultural and Intellectual Property (ICIP)

When working with patterns involving Indigenous data or knowledge:
- Respect Indigenous data sovereignty
- Follow CARE Principles
- Seek appropriate permissions and consultations
- Acknowledge Traditional Owners and knowledge holders

### Research Ethics

Patterns should support:
- Ethical research practices
- Human research ethics compliance
- Data privacy and protection
- Responsible use of AI and emerging technologies
