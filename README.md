# Research Software Engineering Capacity Enhancement Project (RSE-CEP)

[![DOI](https://img.shields.io/badge/DOI-10.3565%2Fx5f6--mw53-blue)](https://doi.org/10.3565/x5f6-mw53)

> Architectural principles, a technology roadmap, and detailed explanations of common RSE patterns for the HASS and Indigenous research community.

## 📋 Overview

The Research Software Engineering Capacity Enhancement Project (RSE-CEP) provides updated architectural principles, a technology roadmap, and detailed explanations of common Research Software Engineering (RSE) patterns for the Humanities, Arts, Social Sciences (HASS) and Indigenous research community. This project is part of the [ARDC HASS and Indigenous Research Data Commons Community Data Lab (CDL)](https://ardc.edu.au/project/ardc-community-data-lab/).


## 🎯 The Challenge

The HASS and Indigenous research community faces a critical shortage of Research Software Engineering expertise, resulting in:

- **Fragmented practices**: Inconsistent software development approaches across disciplines
- **Limited knowledge sharing**: Poor dissemination of tools and techniques despite individual progress
- **Lack of guidance**: Insufficient access to architectural guidance, technical roadmaps, and recommended patterns
- **Sustainability concerns**: Difficulty maintaining research software beyond individual projects
- **Security risks**: Increased cybersecurity vulnerabilities due to inconsistent practices
- **Emerging technology challenges**: Need for responsible integration of AI and Large Language Models in research contexts

Without coordinated efforts to build RSE capability and establish common methods, the HASS and Indigenous research community will struggle to produce innovative, reproducible, secure, and sustainable digital research outputs.

## 💡 The Response

This repository provides three core resources to address these challenges:

### 1. **Architecture** 
Architectural principles and guidance for designing robust research software systems that meet the unique needs of HASS and Indigenous research contexts.

### 2. **Patterns**
Detailed explanations of common RSE patterns, including:
- Pattern definition framework
- Template for creating new patterns
- Practical examples (Configuration Management, Version Control, and more)
- Solutions that balance competing requirements while respecting Indigenous data sovereignty, ethical codes, and research principles

### 3. **Roadmap**
Technology roadmaps to guide strategic planning and implementation of research software infrastructure.

## 👥 Who Will Benefit

This project is designed to support:

- **HASS and Indigenous researchers** using custom software-based data collection, processing, storage, analysis, and publishing methods
- **'Researchers-who-code'** and Research Software Engineers (RSEs) in the HASS and Indigenous sector
- **Software Engineers** working in HASS and Indigenous research environments
- **GLAM sector** (Galleries, Libraries, Archives, Museums) technical managers and staff

## 🎓 Target Outcomes

- **Enhanced capability**: Increased software engineering capability in the HASS and Indigenous research sector
- **Greater collaboration**: Improved sharing of technical solutions and patterns across the community
- **Community building**: Creation of a recognizable working group around these foundational resources
- **Improved interoperability**: Better technical collaboration between research and GLAM sectors
